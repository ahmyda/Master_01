I. Building Blocks of Sentences

Understanding sentence structure begins with knowing the basic components:

Component Definition
Sentence A complete thought with subject and verb, starting with capital letter and ending with punctuation.
Clause Contains subject and verb, but may be only part of a sentence.
Phrase Group of words without finite verb, used as part of a sentence.

Key Distinction: A clause contains both a subject and a complete verb, while a phrase does not contain a finite verb.

Note that:

a. A simple sentence may contain a phrase, but not a (subordinate) clause.

b. The difference between a clause and a phrase is that a clause necessarily contains both a subject and a (complete) verb, unlike a phrase.

c. In a simple sentence, a phrase may be used as:

  • an additional extension to the main clause, e.g. the above appositive phrase "War and Peace," participle phrase "Reading War and Peace," and the prepositional phrases.
  • A replacement of one of the main clause's elements: subject, verb, object, e.g. the above noun phrases, gerund phrase, adverb phrase, adjective phrase, and infinitive phrase.

d. A main clause and a phrase can be combined with a comma (,), a colon (:), or no punctuation mark at all.

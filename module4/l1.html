What is a Research Proposal?

The research proposal is a written document that communicates what you propose to research, in a concise format. It's where you put all that information that's spinning around in your head down on paper, in a logical, convincing way.

Primary Purpose
To help an investigator think through all aspects of the study and anticipate potential problems before conducting the research.
Evaluation Function
Provides a written document that faculty and advisers can read, evaluate, and critique to improve the study design.
Persuasive Document
The research plan or proposal becomes a document to "sell" the study—a written narrative that must convince the faculty of the need, importance, and value of the proposed study.
"A proposal provides a written document that faculty and advisers can read, evaluate, and critique to improve a study. The research plan becomes a document to sell the study."
- Research Methodology Principle
Key Insight

A well-written research proposal demonstrates your understanding of research methodology, your ability to plan systematically, and the significance of your proposed study.

Research Proposal Format (Quantitative)

Quantitative research proposals follow a structured format that emphasizes hypothesis testing, measurement, and statistical analysis.

Quantitative Proposal Structure:

1. Introduction
Overview of the research area and context.
2. Statement of the Problem
Clear identification of the issue and its significance.
3. Purpose of the Study and Delimitations
Objectives and scope boundaries of the research.
4. Theoretical Perspective
Theoretical framework guiding the study.
5. Research Questions or Hypotheses
Specific questions or testable predictions.
6. Review of the Literature
Critical analysis of existing research.
7. Methods
Detailed research methodology section.

Methods Section Details (Quantitative):

Component Description Purpose
Type of Research Design Experimental, correlational, descriptive, etc. Establish research approach
Population, Sample, and Participants Target population and sampling methods Define study subjects
Data Collection Instruments Questionnaires, tests, scales, measurements Specify measurement tools
Data Analysis Procedures Statistical tests and analysis methods Outline analysis approach
Anticipated Ethical Issues Privacy, consent, potential risks Address ethical considerations
Preliminary Studies or Pilot Tests Initial testing of instruments/methods Validate research design
Appendix Requirements

Quantitative proposals typically include appendices with: research instruments, detailed timeline, and proposed budget.

Research Proposal Format (Qualitative)

Qualitative research proposals emphasize understanding phenomena in their natural settings, focusing on depth rather than breadth.

Qualitative Proposal Structure:

Introduction
Context and overview of the qualitative inquiry.
Statement of the Problem
Including existing literature about the problem and significance of the study.
Purpose of the Study
Objectives and how the study will be delimited.
The Research Questions
Open-ended questions guiding the inquiry.
Research Design and Philosophical Assumptions
Methodological approach and underlying paradigms.
Data Collection Procedures
Methods for gathering qualitative data.
Strategies for Validating Findings
Trustworthiness and credibility measures.
Anticipated Ethical Issues
Ethical considerations in qualitative research.
Expected Outcomes
Potential findings and contributions.
"Qualitative research proposals emphasize depth, context, and understanding of phenomena in their natural settings rather than measurement and generalization."
- Qualitative Research Principle
Qualitative Appendix Contents

Typically includes: interview protocols, observational forms, detailed timeline, and proposed budget.

Research Proposal Format (Mixed Methods)

Mixed methods proposals integrate both quantitative and qualitative approaches, leveraging the strengths of both paradigms.

Mixed Methods Proposal Structure:

1. Introduction
Overview and rationale for mixed methods approach.
2. The Research Problem
Statement of the issue requiring mixed methods.
3. Purpose of the Study
Objectives of the integrated approach.
4. Aim of the Study and Objectives
Specific goals and targets.
5. The Research Questions and Hypotheses
Both qualitative and quantitative inquiries.
6. Literature Review
Review of relevant mixed methods studies.
7. Research Design and Philosophical Foundations
Justification for using mixed methods.
8. Data Collection Tools
Both quantitative and qualitative instruments.
9. Data Collection Analysis Procedures
Integration of analytical approaches.
10. Validity Approaches
For both quantitative and qualitative components.
11. Potential Ethical Issues
Ethical considerations in mixed methods.
12. Timeline for Completing the Study
Integrated schedule for both approaches.
13. References and Appendixes
With instruments and visuals.
Integration Challenge

The key challenge in mixed methods proposals is clearly explaining how quantitative and qualitative components will be integrated throughout the research process.

Detailed Proposal Components

Each section of a research proposal serves a specific purpose and must be carefully developed to create a coherent and convincing document.

1. Introduction/Background of the Study

Research Problem
Clear statement of the issue to be investigated.
Overview of Broad Area
Introduce key concepts, terminology, and context.
Specific Focus Area
Explanation of the narrower focus and rationale for this focus.

2. Scope

Defines the boundaries of the research—what will and will not be included in the study.

3. Statement of Problem

Articulates the specific problem the research aims to address, often demonstrating a "gap" in existing knowledge.

4. Significance of the Study

Explains why the research matters—its potential contributions to theory, practice, policy, or methodology.

5. Research Questions and Hypotheses

Main Research Questions
Primary questions guiding the inquiry.
Sub-questions (if applicable)
Supporting questions that break down main questions.
Hypotheses
Testable predictions (primarily in quantitative studies).

6. Research Aims and Objectives

Clear statements of what the research intends to accomplish, often framed as specific, measurable goals.

7. Literature Review

Critical analysis of existing research related to the topic, demonstrating knowledge of the field and identifying gaps.

8. Research Methodology

This critical section must answer two fundamental questions:

Question What to Include Purpose
How will you design your research? Research methodology, sampling strategy, data collection methods, data analysis procedures Describe the research process
Why have you chosen this design? Justification for methodological choices in relation to research aims, objectives, and questions Provide rationale for decisions
Data Collection
Detailed description of how data will be gathered, including instruments, procedures, and timing.
Data Analysis
Explanation of how data will be processed, analyzed, and interpreted.
Resources
Identification of necessary resources (equipment, software, access, funding).
Proposal Evaluation Criteria

A strong research proposal demonstrates: clear research questions, appropriate methodology, feasibility, ethical awareness, and potential for significant contribution to the field.

Proposal Development Checklist

Use this checklist to ensure your research proposal is comprehensive and well-structured.

Introduction & Background
✓ Clear research problem identified
✓ Broad area properly introduced
✓ Specific focus and rationale explained
Research Framework
✓ Scope clearly defined
✓ Problem statement articulated
✓ Significance established
✓ Research questions/hypotheses formulated
✓ Aims and objectives specified
Literature Review
✓ Relevant literature surveyed
✓ Critical analysis performed
✓ Research gap identified
Methodology
✓ Research design selected and justified
✓ Sampling strategy described
✓ Data collection methods detailed
✓ Data analysis procedures explained
✓ Ethical issues anticipated
✓ Resources identified
Supplementary Materials
✓ Timeline developed
✓ Budget proposed (if required)
✓ Appendices prepared (instruments, forms)
✓ References properly formatted
"A well-structured research proposal not only plans your study but also demonstrates your capability as a researcher to think systematically and critically about your topic."

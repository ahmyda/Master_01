1. Identifying Research Problems

Purpose of Academic Problems

Academic problems aim to create objective and reliable new knowledge through systematic investigation.

  • Can be questions, knowledge gaps, or descriptive attempts
  • Must generate researchable questions
  • Often start from abstract ideas
  • Unseen variables emerge during formulation
Problem Selection Process

Identifying a research problem is time-consuming and involves careful consideration.

  • Selecting a broad area of interest
  • Narrowing down the focus
  • Limiting the scope
  • Conceptualizing the entire work

Example: Online teaching during COVID-19 could include subtopics like advantages, drawbacks, obstacles, attitudes, and effectiveness reports.

2. Review of Literature

Purposes of Literature Review

A literature review is a crucial step in the research process that serves multiple purposes throughout the research journey.

  • Helps identify research problems
  • Determines feasibility of the study
  • Facilitates conceptualization
  • Provides written summary of current knowledge
  • Refines research methodology
  • Offers state-of-the-art methods and techniques
  • Provides solid conceptual framework

3. Formulating a Hypothesis

Hypothesis Definition

Specific statements derived from research questions that anticipate relationships between variables.

  • Provides specificity to scientific studies
  • Derived from research questions
  • Anticipates variable relationships
Quantitative Research

Often includes explicit hypotheses that guide the research design and data analysis.

  • Hypotheses are typically formulated early
  • Guide research design
  • Provide focus for data collection
Qualitative Research

Allows hypotheses to emerge gradually throughout the research process rather than being predetermined.

  • Hypotheses may emerge during research
  • More flexible approach
  • Adapts to emerging findings

4. Research Design

Definition

According to Claire Selltiz (1960):

"A research design is the arrangement of conditions for collection and analysis of data in a manner that aims to combine relevance to the research purpose with economy in procedure."

Key Aspects

Research design involves crucial decisions that should be considered from the beginning of the study.

  • Limiting research questions
  • Minimizing bias
  • Maximizing data reliability
  • Choosing samples and sampling techniques
  • Deciding on data analysis methods

5. Collecting Data

Beyond Information Gathering

Data collection is more than just gathering information - it's a process of developing sound evidence to support arguments.

"The authors collect certain information in certain ways from or about certain people or settings, and the research report argues that this information may be interpreted in certain ways to lead to true conclusions about a certain population." - Sapsford & Jupp (2006)

Importance of Data Collection (Singh, 2006)

  1. Provides a solid foundation for educational research
  2. Acts as raw material for data production - quality determines research quality
  3. Needed to demonstrate various arguments in research findings
  4. Main purpose is to verify hypotheses
  5. Necessary to provide solutions to research problems

6. Data Analysis and Interpretation

Data Analysis

Critical process of cleaning, transforming, and modeling raw data to make it usable.

  • Correcting errors, duplication, and deviations
  • Removing irrelevant data
  • Transforming into usable format
  • Revealing hidden patterns and relationships
  • Using visualizations: graphs, dashboards, tables
Data Interpretation

Process of summarizing, categorizing, and manipulating data to reach relevant conclusions.

  • Summarizing and categorizing data
  • Drawing meaningful conclusions
  • More subjective than analysis
  • Often used interchangeably with analysis but distinct

7. Report Writing

Standard Research Report Structure
  1. Title - Clear, concise description of the study
  2. Abstract - Brief summary of the entire study
  3. Introduction - Presents the research problem and context
  4. Literature review - Summary of relevant existing research
  5. Method - Detailed description of research procedures
  6. Results - Presentation of research findings
  7. Discussion - Interpretation and implications of results
  8. References - List of all cited sources
  9. Appendixes - Supplementary materials

Characteristics: Formal language, third-person perspective, objective, accurate, ethical, follows consistent structure.

Critical Thinking Questions

Discussion Points:

  • How does the research process ensure systematic and reliable knowledge creation?
  • What challenges might researchers face when narrowing down a broad research topic?
  • Why is literature review considered a foundational step in the research process?
  • How do hypotheses differ between quantitative and qualitative research approaches?
  • What role does research design play in minimizing bias and maximizing reliability?

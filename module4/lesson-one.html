Research Methodology

Research Steps

Research Process Overview

  1. Review the Literature: Study existing research to understand what has already been done and identify gaps.
  2. Define Research Problem: Clearly state the problem or question that your research aims to address.
  3. Formulate Hypotheses: Create testable statements or predictions based on your research question.
  4. Design Research: Plan your methodology, including sample selection, tools, and techniques.
  5. Collect Data: Gather the information needed according to your research design.
  6. Analyse Data: Examine the collected data and test your hypotheses.
  7. Interpret & Report: Summarize findings, draw conclusions, and present the results in a report.

1. Identifying Research Problems

Purpose of Academic Problems

Create objective and reliable knowledge through systematic investigation.

  • Can be questions, gaps, or descriptive attempts
  • Must generate researchable questions
  • Often start from abstract ideas
  • Unseen variables emerge during formulation
Problem Selection Process
  • Select broad area of interest
  • Narrow down focus
  • Limit scope
  • Conceptualize entire work

Example: Online teaching during COVID-19 with subtopics: advantages, drawbacks, obstacles, attitudes, effectiveness.

2. Review of Literature

Purposes of Literature Review
  • Helps identify research problems
  • Determines feasibility
  • Facilitates conceptualization
  • Provides written summary of knowledge
  • Refines methodology
  • Offers state-of-the-art methods
  • Provides conceptual framework

3. Formulating a Hypothesis

Hypothesis Definition
  • Specific statements from research questions
  • Provides specificity
  • Anticipates relationships
Quantitative Research
  • Hypotheses formulated early
  • Guide design
  • Focus data collection
Qualitative Research
  • Hypotheses emerge gradually
  • Flexible approach
  • Adapts to findings

4. Research Design

Definition

"Arrangement of conditions for collection and analysis of data combining relevance and economy." - Claire Selltiz (1960)

Key Aspects
  • Limit research questions
  • Minimize bias
  • Maximize reliability
  • Choose samples & techniques
  • Decide data analysis methods

5. Collecting Data

Beyond Information Gathering

Data collection develops evidence to support research arguments.

"Collected info may be interpreted to lead to true conclusions." - Sapsford & Jupp (2006)

6. Data Analysis and Interpretation

Data Analysis
  • Correct errors & remove irrelevant data
  • Transform data into usable format
  • Reveal patterns & relationships
  • Use visualizations
Data Interpretation
  • Summarize & categorize data
  • Draw conclusions
  • More subjective than analysis
  • Distinct from analysis

7. Report Writing

Standard Research Report Structure
  1. Title - Clear, concise description
  2. Abstract - Summary of study
  3. Introduction - Research problem & context
  4. Literature review
  5. Method - Procedures
  6. Results
  7. Discussion
  8. References
  9. Appendixes

Characteristics: Formal, objective, ethical, accurate, structured.

Critical Thinking Questions
  • How does the research process ensure systematic knowledge?
  • Challenges in narrowing a broad topic?
  • Why is literature review foundational?
  • Difference in hypotheses between quantitative & qualitative?
  • Role of research design in bias & reliability?

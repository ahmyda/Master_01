Effective Teaching

  • Having high expectations for all students
  • Acknowledging individual differences in learning styles and abilities
  • Monitoring progress and providing timely, constructive feedback
  • Encouraging student responsibility for their own learning
  • Building positive relationships with students and colleagues
Note: These attitudes and abilities can be undermined by various factors.

Constraints in Effective Teaching

Effective teaching is crucial for fostering student success, but several factors can undermine a teacher's ability to create an optimal learning environment, including:

  • The diversity of learning styles among students
  • Varied ability levels within the same classroom
  • Insufficient teaching materials and resources
  • Overwhelming workloads and administrative tasks
  • Lack of professional development opportunities

Teachers' Beliefs

What do we mean by "belief"?

A belief is a kind of knowledge that is subjective and experience-based. Raymond (1997) defined the term belief as a personal judgment formed from experiences.

EFL teachers' beliefs are personal constructs of their practical EFL learning and teaching experiences, and these beliefs are also formulated as a result of their experience and interaction with their social and cultural context (Abelson 1979; Connelly and Clandinin 1988).

Teachers' beliefs are very difficult to define and evaluate.

Why are teachers' beliefs difficult to define and evaluate?

  1. Culturally Bound
    • Teachers' beliefs about education, teaching methods, and student behavior are shaped by:
      • The culture they grew up in
      • The educational system they experienced
      • The cultural norms of their society
  2. Formed Early in Life
    • Teachers' beliefs often stem from their own schooling experiences.
  3. Resistant to Change
    • Teachers' beliefs are usually formed over many years—through personal schooling experiences, training, teaching practice, and interactions with colleagues, students, and parents.

Teachers' Beliefs About Learners

The Influence of Teachers' Beliefs About Learners on Classroom Practices

Teachers' beliefs about learners significantly shape their views on education and have a direct impact on classroom practices.

Resisters

Some students may be viewed as resistant to learning. This belief can lead to the assumption that force or punishment is the most effective way to overcome resistance.

Receptacles

In this model, students are seen as passive "receptacles" that need to be filled with knowledge. The emphasis is on direct transmission of information from teacher to student.

Raw Materials

Students can be molded or shaped into finished products. This approach carries the risk of manipulating students according to predetermined ideas rather than allowing them to develop their own identities.

Partners

In this approach, the teacher and students are regarded as partners in the learning process. Teachers emphasize collaboration and joint discovery over traditional hierarchy.

Individual Explorers

In such cases the teacher acts as a facilitator rather than the primary source of knowledge, supporting students in their individual learning journeys.

Teachers' Beliefs About Learning

"The question of what makes a good teacher must ultimately be concerned with what and how and how much learners learn and what exactly that learning is for."

Teachers can truly be effective if they are clear in their mind about what learning is. They will then know what kinds of learning outcomes they want their learners to achieve.

These beliefs about learning deeply affect classroom dynamics, as they guide the strategies teachers use to engage and support their students in meaningful, effective ways.

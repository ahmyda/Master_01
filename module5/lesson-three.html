Effective Teaching

  • Having high expectations for all students
  • Acknowledging individual differences in learning styles and abilities
  • Monitoring progress and providing timely, constructive feedback
  • Encouraging student responsibility for their own learning
  • Building positive relationships with students and colleagues
Note: These attitudes and abilities can be undermined by various factors.

Constraints in Effective Teaching

Effective teaching is crucial for student success, but factors can limit its effectiveness:

  • Diversity of learning styles
  • Varied ability levels in the classroom
  • Insufficient teaching materials and resources
  • Overwhelming workloads and administrative tasks
  • Lack of professional development

Teachers' Beliefs

Definition of Belief

A belief is a type of knowledge that is subjective and experience-based. It is a personal judgment formed from experience (Raymond, 1997).

EFL teachers' beliefs are personal constructs of their practical learning and teaching experiences, shaped by social and cultural context (Abelson 1979; Connelly & Clandinin 1988).

Teachers' beliefs are difficult to define and evaluate.

Why Beliefs Are Hard to Evaluate

  1. Culturally Bound – influenced by the culture, education system, and norms teachers grew up with
  2. Formed Early – based on personal schooling experiences
  3. Resistant to Change – shaped over many years through experience, training, and interactions

Beliefs About Learners

Teachers' beliefs influence classroom practices and affect how students are perceived and taught.

Resisters

Viewed as resistant to learning; may rely on force or punishment.

Receptacles

Seen as passive vessels needing direct knowledge transmission.

Raw Materials

Molded into finished products; risks limiting students’ own development.

Partners

Teacher and students collaborate in learning.

Individual Explorers

Teacher acts as facilitator for independent learning journeys.

Beliefs About Learning

"What makes a good teacher must ultimately be concerned with what and how learners learn and why."

Teachers are most effective when clear about learning outcomes and how to achieve them.

Beliefs about learning guide strategies, engagement, and support for students in meaningful ways.

Steps and Importance of Needs Analysis

Steps in Needs Analysis (Brown, 1995)

Step 1: Making Basic Decisions

Before any needs analysis study takes place, researchers must make certain decisions about:

  • Target Group: People about whom information will be collected
  • Audience: People who will apply the results (teachers, administrators)
  • Analysts: Those responsible for conducting the needs analysis
  • Resource Group: People who serve as sources of information

Step 2: Gathering Information

Various techniques for collecting data:

  • Tests: General ability levels and specific language problems
  • Observations: Watching and recording student behaviors
  • Interviews: Gathering personal information and views
  • Meetings: Reaching consensus among stakeholders
  • Questionnaires: Efficient for large-scale information gathering
  • Existing Information: Review of available records

Step 3: Using Collected Data

Data analysis considerations:

  • Reliability: Consistency of the procedure
  • Validity: Degree to which instrument measures what it claims
  • Usability: Practicality of the procedure

Importance of Needs Analysis

Course Design Foundation

Berwick (1989) states that needs assessment is important for decision planners to design the course. McDonough (1984) asserts that "information on his or her language needs will help in drawing up a profile to establish coherent objectives, and take subsequent decisions on course content".

Syllabus and Materials Development

Riddell (1991) points to the crucial role that needs analysis plays in syllabus and course design. Jordan (1997) states that "needs analysis should be the starting point for devising syllabus, course materials and classroom activities".

Core Principle of ESP

One of the main principles of ESP says: "tell me what you 'need' English for and I will tell you the English that you 'need'" (Hutchinson and Waters, 1987). ESP places great emphasis on needs analysis at every stage: course designing, learning/teaching process and application.

Continuous Process

Needs analysis should be carried out throughout the course in order to adjust the learning objectives as the need arises. Feedback from learners can be used as basis for modifying learning objectives.
